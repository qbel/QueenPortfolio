Design Steps

I follow this steps

  • 1

    Research

  • 2

    Prototype

  • 3

    Design

  • 4

    Development

  • 5

    Result

Research Research really begins with asking the right research questions, what follows next is choosing appropriate research method to be able to investigate the problem or issue in the right direction and then finally analyze your findings or observations to draw appropriate conclusions.
Prototype A prototype is generally used to evaluate a new design to enhance precision by system analysts and users. In some design workflow models, creating a prototype is the step between the formalization and the evaluation of an idea.
Design This involves giving life to the researched idea and making it look real and achieveable. It also involves seeing through the entire project as the client and understanding the project from the perspective of the client.
Development Building on the blocks after researching and coming up with ideas on how to go about the entire process is what is termed development. This involves practically starting up the project based on the prototype designed.
Result This is what is seen as the end result of all the processes mentioned earlier. If the processes are executed corrected you either get the desired output or a different one based on how you understand the clients explaination(s).
